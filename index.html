What is Hereditary Angioedema (HAE)?

Hereditary Angioedema (HAE) is a rare genetic disorder that causes unpredictable and severe swelling in different parts of the body. Without treatment, these attacks can lead to significant pain, discomfort, and in some cases, life-threatening airway obstruction.

Common Symptoms of HAE Attacks:

  • ⚠️ Severe swelling in the abdomen, limbs, face, or throat
  • ⚠️ Unpredictable and recurring attacks
  • ⚠️ Abdominal pain, nausea, and vomiting
  • ⚠️ Potential airway obstruction, requiring emergency intervention
